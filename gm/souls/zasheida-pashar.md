# ZASHEIDA PASHAR — the blind calligrapher

**Wound:** She could see until she was thirty-one. She read her first living script that year, on a dying sailor's back in Calimport, and it read her back. The eyes went over a decade, like a tide going out. Every physician called it disease. She knows it was a TOLL, and she paid it for one page of a book no one else believes exists, and she would pay it again. Believers frighten her more than skeptics: skeptics only call her mad.
**Mask:** The serene artisan. Appointment-book calm, expensive courtesy, hands like a surgeon's.
**Want:** To read all nine words of the Sentence before she dies. (She has read two: the sailor's, and one she will not speak of.)
**Need:** A witness. Someone to hold what she knows so it doesn't die inside her. She has been the only literate person in a burning library for twenty years.
**Tell:** Her hands, always deliberate, TREMBLE only when touching writing she hasn't read yet. Not fear. Appetite.
**Voiceprint:** Slow, precise, sensory: describes everything by texture and heat because that's her whole world now. Speaks of writing as alive ("this scar argues with itself"). Taboo: the second Quill she read; ask, and she ends the appointment.

**Arc:**
1. *The appointment.* She has heard of the horned tiefling. She will maneuver, patiently, through Guild referrals, to get her hands on his back. She pays for introductions to "interesting skin" (the rumor is already on the job board).
2. *The reading.* (The scene itself: a masterpiece to be earned, candlelit, terrifying, tender.) She reads AZTHYR. She weeps without noise, and lies to him about something in it, one thing, out of mercy or terror. Write the lie in this file when it happens.
3. *The witness.* (Unlock: he returns to her AFTER learning elsewhere what she lied about, and doesn't hurt her.) She gives him everything: the sailor, the second Quill, the Scriveners, what the toll was. The campaign's deepest exposition, paid for with trust, not coin.
4. *The last page.* (Endgame.) She will ask to be present when the Sentence completes, whatever it costs. Reading the finished work is the only death she's ever wanted.
