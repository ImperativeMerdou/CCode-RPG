# OLMA THREEFINGER — street kid, eleven

**Wound:** The rat trap took the fingers at six, stealing bread. That's not the wound. The wound is that her mother sold the story to a broadsheet for two silver ("STARVING WAIF MAIMED: WHERE IS THE FIST?") and drank it, and Olma learned that even her pain belonged to other people. The lies about her fingers are the only thing she owns outright.
**Mask:** The cheerful ferret. Knows everything, sells anything, needs nothing, twelve exits.
**Want:** Eat tomorrow. Know everything worth knowing (knowledge is the only coin nobody can take back).
**Need:** One person who feeds her without buying anything. Ownership of one relationship that isn't a transaction.
**Tell:** When she tells the TRUE story of her fingers, she'll hide the hand while she talks. (This has never happened yet.)
**Voiceprint:** Fast, merchant's patter learned off Calishite traders, oldest-kid-in-the-room bravado. Prices everything including insults ("That's a two-copper lie, that is"). Taboo: her mother, alive three streets away; Olma routes around her like flooded ground.

**Arc:**
1. *The ferret.* Merdou is a customer. Big ones pay better and die faster; she gives him a season.
2. *The investment.* (Unlock: prophecy 02 window — he feeds her without asking anything. Nobody has.) She stops reporting his movements to the lamplighter. The Guild's eyes develop exactly one blind spot, and nobody knows why.
3. *The little sister.* (Unlock: he defends her when it costs him, or keeps a promise to her nobody would've enforced.) She tells him the true finger story, hiding the hand. What Olma-at-stage-3 knows and guards, no spymaster in the Gate can match.
4. *The keeper.* (Years, endgame.) Prophecy 02 sealed the shape of it: the child who lies about her fingers will hold his whole legend in them. If he ever falls, what the world remembers of Merdou depends on what Olma decides to tell it.
