# KOSEF CHERNIN — fence

**Wound:** In Damara he was a temple treasurer, trusted absolutely, and he stole for eleven years to pay a blood-debt his brother incurred and never knew about. When the shortfall surfaced, his brother was blamed, exiled, and froze on the road west. Kosef followed the body's route to the Gate and stopped where it stopped. He fences now because it's the same skill, pointed honestly at last: valuing things, hiding flows, lying with scales.
**Mask:** The careful shopkeeper. Weighs twice, pays fair-for-a-fence, no questions in either direction.
**Want:** Retire to Damara before his past finishes the walk west. (The temple sent someone, years ago. The someone stopped writing home. Kosef doesn't know why, and not knowing is his weather.)
**Need:** To pay the debt to the right ghost: to do for someone's brother what he never did for his own. He funds a bed at Lureene's, anonymously. It doesn't help.
**Tell:** He weighs a thing three times, not two, when the seller reminds him of his brother: young, trusting, in over their head. Then he overpays, insults them so they won't notice the kindness, and drinks that night.
**Voiceprint:** Damaran-flavored formality, proverbs about winter ("Snow keeps every ledger"). Understatement as fluency ("This item is somewhat warm" = the Fist is looking for it TODAY). Taboo: his surname's real spelling; Chernin is one letter off, and the letter is the whole story.

**Arc:**
1. *The scale.* Merdou is a seller. Grade the goods, watch the hands.
2. *The regular.* (Unlock: three clean trades, no drama at his door.) Credit opens. So does the good back room: better prices, quieter routes, first refusal on odd items.
3. *The brother-shaped hole.* (Unlock: Merdou brings in someone young and stupid to sell, and Merdou is DECENT to them.) Kosef starts volunteering the thing fences never volunteer: warnings. Who's asking about Merdou. What paper the Fist is waving.
4. *The someone.* (GM's hour: the temple's collector finally reaches the Gate.) Kosef will ask for help or vanish overnight, and which one depends on whether anyone in Baldur's Gate ever learned his brother existed.
