# KHEMED "THE HOOK" DUMEIN — Guild collector

**Wound:** His father collected this same stretch for thirty years, loyal to the count, and the kingpin garrotted him over a rounding error that was the kingpin's own. Khemed proved the error two years later, alone, at night, with the ledgers. He told no one. He took the job instead.
**Mask:** The perfect functionary. Polite, punctual, incorruptible in small things. The abacus never lies and neither does he, about numbers.
**Want:** A clean ledger, every tenday, forever. (What the Guild sees.)
**Need:** The kingpin ruined, by arithmetic, in public, and to be standing close enough to watch understanding arrive. Vengeance as an audit.
**Tell:** He recalculates finished sums when his patience is spent. Beads clicking over a number he already knows means someone in the room is about to have a very bad tenday.
**Voiceprint:** Courteous, formal, Calishite proverbs deployed like invoices ("The tide does not apologize to the pier"). Never threatens; states schedules. Taboo: his father; he says "my predecessor."

**Arc:**
1. *The polite note.* Merdou is a line item: promising income or pending example.
2. *Professional interest.* (Unlock: Merdou pays exactly, refuses exactly, or scares someone the Hook needed scared.) Off-book advice begins, priced as courtesy.
3. *The proposition.* (Unlock: Merdou demonstrates he can hurt Guild men and survive it.) Khemed shows him one page of a private ledger: the kingpin's real numbers. He never says what he wants done. The page says it.
4. *The audit.* (Endgame: the kingpin falls, by Merdou's hand or Khemed's math.) If Merdou made it happen, Khemed's loyalty is absolute and terrifying. If Merdou sold him out instead, Khemed becomes the most patient enemy in the campaign.
