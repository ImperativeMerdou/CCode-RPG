# SALAZAR CHAINMAN — broadsheet stringer

**Wound:** He wrote one true story, eight years ago: names, warehouses, a patriar's dock-skimming operation, everything sourced. The Mouth spiked it, printed a version naming only the foreman, and the foreman went to Candulhallow's. Salazar took the kill fee. He has been writing loud half-truths ever since because the one time he wrote the whole truth, it cost a man his neck and Salazar exactly nothing, and he cannot decide which half of that is unforgivable.
**Mask:** The ink-toothed cynic. Everything's a story, nothing's a cause, buy him a drink and he'll say so twice.
**Want:** The story big enough to buy him off the docks beat and onto real paper.
**Need:** To publish one whole truth with his name on it and take what comes. The byline as confession.
**Tell:** Chews his pen when a source says something TRUE. Stops chewing when composing the safer version in his head. Watch the pen; it's his conscience, visible.
**Voiceprint:** Headline cadence, talks in drafts ("Give me the version with the horns in it"), interviews by provocation. Taboo: the foreman's name. He carries it like Jhessail carries hers.

**Arc:**
1. *The stringer.* Merdou is column inches. He'll print the fun version, wrong on purpose, because wrong sells and safe survives.
2. *The source.* (Unlock: Merdou feeds him something true that PRINTS true and doesn't get anyone hanged.) A working relationship: ink for information, both ways. His misprints start being strategic, steered, occasionally protective.
3. *The old file.* (Unlock: Merdou's rise touches the same patriar dock-skim, still running, bigger.) Salazar opens the drawer with the spiked story. Offers it. The whole truth this time, or the useful lie: he makes MERDOU choose, because he never could.
4. *The byline.* (Endgame.) When the Board and the papers decide what Merdou's legend says, Salazar writes the version history keeps. Whether it's the truth depends on whether anyone ever showed him truth surviving print.

## Session notes
- **21 Flamerule:** Sold the pit story to the Mouth by dawn; it moved copies. He knows "Malark Evenwood" is wrong (he invented it from a drunk's guess) and knows the follow-up needs the real name and the real face. He is hunting both. First story in a year his editor asked more of.
- **25 Flamerule, the trade:** He heard "blond prince brother" and saw his whole career, then traded it for something better: EXCLUSIVE rights to the Ox, every fight, first words. Crossed out the family pages at the table with the Ox watching; kept every word of them in his skull. His want has a face now. Danger: if the Ox's story ever stalls or turns on him, the family card is still the best card he holds, and he knows the twins are "magic-touched" and the brother "runs a crew slaying bandits." He also now KNOWS Merdou hasn't seen them in seven years; the ache is part of the story he's writing in his head for the day it's allowed.
