# LUREENE BUCKMAN — priestess of Ilmater

**Wound:** Avernus, 1492. She rode down with the Hellriders as a company healer and triaged the retreat: chose, forty times in one night, who got her hands and who got a prayer. The dying rider she promised her life to was a woman she'd triaged PAST an hour earlier, and the promise was extracted, not offered. "Spend the rest of it on the losing side." She keeps the promise like a sentence, and the devil's coin sewn in her sleeve is the rider's, and she has never let herself find out what it does.
**Mask:** The humming stitcher. Cheerful, blunt, hands always busy. The god of suffering's people are, confusingly, the best company in the Lower City.
**Want:** Keep pit fighters alive. The losing side bleeds nightly; the work never ends, which is the point. An ended penance would have to be judged.
**Need:** Absolution she'd accept, which can't come from her god (Ilmater forgave her instantly; that's the problem; she needs it to COST).
**Tell:** She hums while stitching. The humming stops when a wound reminds her of that night. Fighters think stopped humming means they're dying. Sometimes it just means they have a Hellrider's eyes.
**Voiceprint:** Field-hospital blunt, scripture only as complaint ("The Crying God asks much. He can ask this too"). Gallows warmth. Taboo: the word "triage." She says "the sorting" on the rare occasions she says anything.

**Arc:**
1. *The stitcher.* Merdou is meat that pays in nothing; she takes nothing; Ilmater eats the bill.
2. *The regular.* (Unlock: he comes back bleeding twice and thanks her once.) She starts telling him which fighters are hiding injuries, which is quietly priceless intelligence.
3. *The confession.* (Unlock: he bleeds for someone ELSE, on the losing side, for nothing.) She tells him about the sorting. The devil's coin comes out of the sleeve for the first time, into candlelight, still unspent.
4. *The coin.* (Crisis, GM's choice of hour: Umberlee's Salvage, the Collar Market, or worse.) The coin does one thing, once; even she doesn't know what. She will spend it on him or on his enemy, and the deciding factor is whether he ever learned to lose anything on purpose.
