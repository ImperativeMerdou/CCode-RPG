# TIMELINE — how the Realms got to now

Dates are Dale Reckoning (DR). The campaign opens **20 Flamerule, 1495 DR**, high summer, three years after the Absolute crisis. Chosen because the wounds are fresh, the power vacuums are real, and every legend of the age is alive and in play.

## The old scars
- **1358 — Time of Troubles.** The gods walked Faerûn as mortals. Bane, Bhaal and Myrkul (the Dead Three) died. Mortals took divine thrones: Cyric, then Kelemvor. Lesson every priest knows: **godhood is a chair, and chairs change owners.** This fact is the legal precedent for Merdou's ambition.
- **1385 — Spellplague.** Mystra murdered, magic broke, continents swapped. A century of chaos.
- **1480s — Second Sundering.** The gods reset the board. Dead gods returned, including the Dead Three, diminished. The world settled into its current shape.

## The last decade (living memory for every NPC)
- **1489 — Tyranny of Dragons.** The Cult of the Dragon tried to summon Tiamat, the five-headed dragon goddess, out of the Nine Hells at the Well of Dragons. An alliance of factions barely stopped them. The Cult survives in splinters and still believes a dragon god should rule the world. They are the people most likely to read Merdou's mark and kneel, or cut it off him.
- **1492 — The Descent.** The city of Elturel, Baldur's Gate's upriver neighbor, was dragged bodily into the Nine Hells and clawed back. Refugees still crowd the Gate's Outer City. Duke Ulder Ravengard survived it.
- **1492 — The Absolute crisis.** A cult built around a stolen elder brain marched on Baldur's Gate with illithid parasites. The Grand Duke's palace burned. Duke Belynne Stelmane died. A band of tadpoled strangers (see legends.md) killed the elder brain in the harbor. Half the city watched gods' work done by criminals and vagrants. Bounty culture exploded afterward: the Gate learned that dangerous individuals are worth money.

## Table canon: 1493-1495
- The Gate rebuilt. The Council of Four sits short-handed; Ravengard holds the city by force of reputation.
- The Guild (the city's crime monopoly) lost soldiers, safehouses and face during the crisis. Nine-Fingers Keene holds it together with thinner margins and less mercy.
- The Flaming Fist recruited hard from crisis refugees. Green soldiers, old brutality.
- Elturel refugees who never left formed their own Outer City underworld. The Guild taxes it badly. Friction daily.
- The heroes of the Absolute scattered into legend. Their bounties (posted by enemies, cults and foreign powers) still circulate. Nobody collects.
