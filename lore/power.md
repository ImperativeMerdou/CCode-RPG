# POWER — the Weight, and the ladder of monsters

## THE WEIGHT (the new force, b. ~1492)

Magic is old. The gods are older. The Weight is FOUR YEARS OLD and nobody, not Candlekeep, not the zulkirs, not the church of Mystra, knows why it started.

The first documented case is from the Absolute crisis: a Fist sergeant on the Wyrm's Crossing barricade, out of spells' reach, out of hope, held a line against cultists that should have rolled him, and the men beside him swear the air BENT. Since then it surfaces coast-wide, a few souls a season: always mortals, always at the edge of will, never through study. Wizards despise it because it cannot be researched. Priests distrust it because it prays to nothing. The street just says a person "carries weight," and the street, as usual, has it closest.

The Weight is will made force. It does not care about bloodlines, spellbooks or gods. It is the one ladder anyone can climb and no one can buy, which is why the young and the hungry believe in it like a religion, and why every throne on the coast quietly fears where it is going.

### The three expressions
**THE LISTENING.** Weight turned outward. Intent has pressure, and the Listener feels it land before the blow does.
- *Stage 1, the Ear:* hostile intent within a room prickles his skin. He cannot be ambushed by anything that means it.
- *Stage 2, the Eye:* he reads the half-second: feints declare themselves, lies arrive as pressure without content, arrows lean.
- *Stage 3, the Long Ear (rare):* two heartbeats ahead. Masters describe fighting a Stage 3 as "arguing with someone who has read your letter."

**THE SET.** Weight turned inward, "setting" will into flesh and steel. The street phrase for a hardened fighter: "he set his arm."
- *Stage 1, the Skin:* his body bites and blocks like a forged thing. Fists and blades he holds can wound creatures that shrug at plain steel: ghosts, devils, things wizards say need enchantment. The Weight disagrees, heavily.
- *Stage 2, the Edge:* blades turn on his hide; his blows land like falling cargo.
- *Stage 3, the Inward Crush (rare):* his strike ignores the shell and speaks to what's inside. Armor, scale, wall. Once a fight, and it costs.

**THE CROWN.** Weight that commands. Born, never trained into existence, one in a hundred thousand: the will of a person the world was going to have to deal with anyway. Kings' blood claims it falsely. Gutters produce it just as often.
- *Latent:* it bursts, unchosen, at moments touching the bearer's deepest line. Rooms go quiet. Weak knees fold. The bearer usually doesn't know what happened.
- *Stage 1, the Voice:* he can flare it: lesser wills (mook-tier) drop or break. Equals feel a hand on the shoulder of their soul.
- *Stage 2, the Presence:* pressure duels. Two Crowns in one room is weather.
- *Stage 3, the Sovereign's Hand (near-myth):* the Crown rides his blows. Perhaps five living souls.

### The costs (always honest)
Using the Weight is spending something real. Each scene of active use past the first stacks strain; strain becomes written exhaustion conditions. Overreach past your stage and the body pays in blood: burst vessels, a day deaf, a numb arm. Stages rise ONLY through training under someone heavier, or through trials that nearly kill. There are perhaps a dozen people on the Sword Coast who can teach Stage 2. Stage 3 teachers can be counted on one maimed hand.

### Who carries weight (known and rumored)
Shautha the Anvil has Set 1 and does not know its name; she calls it "keeping your feet." Duke Ravengard is whispered to carry it since Avernus. The Bonesaw is rumored a Listener. Among the Board's names, assume nothing and fear everything. Merdou: unknown. (See his file. See the vault. See the future.)

## THE LADDER OF MONSTERS (how the coast ranks the dangerous)

The Board's clerks invented the categories; the taverns made them law. Power scaling is HONEST: a fight one category up is desperate, two up is a death sentence without terrain, allies, or a miracle. Nobody skips rungs without paying.

| Category | Bounty band | What it means | Faces |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Named** | to 1,000 | The street knows the name | Keth, Grim Dundragon, Shautha |
| **Knives** | 1k-10k | Professionals; a Named dies to one, usually | the Sexton's buriers, Fist captains, the Bonesaw |
| **Captains** | 10k-100k | Small armies answer to them or run from them | kingpins like the Sexton, Scrivener prophets, Astarion's band |
| **Crowns of the Board** | 100k+ | The six names; cities plan around them | Karlach, Entreri, Jarlaxle, the Xanathar |
| **CALAMITIES** | numbers retired | Not criminals. Weather. Four powers the coast simply endures, each owning a quarter of the map's fear | **Klauth** (the North), **Szass Tam** (the East), **Halaster** (the Below), **Iymrith** (the Sands) |
| **Thrones** | — | The gods. The ladder ends where worship begins | Tempus. Tiamat. The chairs themselves |

The Calamities are the coast's Four Emperors in everything but name: none rules a government, all rule geography. Merchants route around them. Kingdoms pay them without invoices. The Board keeps no numbers on them because no one would survive collecting.

**The scaling law:** every category boundary is real in play. Crossing one is an ARC, never a scene. The engine never hands him a rung; the engine lets him bleed for one.
