# STREET LIFE — the taste of the Lower City

The texture file. Steal from it every scene: two senses minimum, one detail that costs nothing and buys everything.

## Sound
Gulls before dawn, chain-rattle of the harbor cranes, the Basilisk Gate toll bell, dockers' work songs in three languages, the Fist's iron-shod boots (you hear a patrol two streets off), rain on ten thousand slate roofs, the Elfsong's dead voice drifting out at odd hours.

## Smell
Fish guts and tar at the docks. Coal smoke uphill. Cheap lamp oil, wet wool, blood sausage frying at dawn stalls. The Undercellar: candle wax, mildew, perfume over both. After rain, the river smell climbs the Steeps.

## Food and drink (with prices)
- Blood sausage and hard bread, dawn stall: 2cp
- Chionthar eel stew, docker's portion: 3cp
- Suzale (the Gate's rough ale): 1cp the mug. Bitters at the Elfsong: 4cp
- Calishite coffee, Little Calimshan, thick as mud: 5cp and worth it
- A patriar dinner Merdou will not be eating: 5gp a plate

## Oaths and slang (Baldurian mouth)
- "By the Gate" (mild), "Umberlee take it" (sailor's), "Nine bells" (alarm, trouble), "Bhaal's leavings" (filth, worst insult)
- **A "patriar"**: old-money noble. **"Fist-kisser"**: informant. **"Gray cloaks"**: the Watch. **"The nine-fingered discount"**: Guild tax. **"Gone to Candulhallow's"**: dead. **"Wearing the collar"**: indentured. **"A Klauth day"**: weather so bad it feels personal
- Coin slang: "eyes" (coppers), "teeth" (silver), "suns" (gold)

## Small gods of the street
Dockers flip a copper to Tymora before a dangerous lift and drown one for Umberlee before a voyage. Ilmater's broken-hands symbol chalked on a door means free help inside. Hoar's name spoken over a debt means the creditor has stopped counting money and started counting wrongs. Nobody says Bhaal's name after dark. The city remembers being his twice.

## The nights
The Lower City has three: dusk to midnight (commerce, honest and not), midnight to third bell (Guild hours; the Fist patrols in pairs and pretends not to see), third bell to dawn (the hours nobody witnesses anything). Lamplighters are Guild ears. So are half the street kids.

## Weather, Flamerule (high summer)
Heat that makes the fish stink climb the hill. Afternoon thunderheads off the sea. Night storms that kill harbor traffic and tempers. Fights double in Flamerule. Lureene Buckman calls it the bleeding month.
