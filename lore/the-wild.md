# THE WILD — everything the walls keep out

The city is one board. This is the rest. Legend grows faster out here: no witnesses means no bounty, but a monster's head carried through a gate is worth more fame than ten street fights. Distances from Baldur's Gate, times on foot or by cart.

## The near wild (day trips to a tenday)
- **The Coast Way (south) and Trade Way (north).** The arteries. Caravans, toll bridges, patrols near the city thinning to nothing by day three. Caravan guard work: 1gp a day plus meals, the classic first job out of the gate. Bandits price caravans the way the Guild prices shops.
- **Cloak Wood (two days south, coastal).** Old forest, older dark. Wyverns, ettercaps, spider-woods, and a ruined mining complex the Iron Throne abandoned a century back. Loggers work its edge and pay for protection.
- **The River Chionthar.** The Gate's water road. Barge traffic to Elturel and beyond. River pirates work the reed bends. Umberlee's writ runs to the brackish line.
- **Wyrm's Crossing and the Outer Roads.** Bridge-slum, toll men, smugglers' skiffs by night.
- **Durlag's Tower (southeast, in the hills).** The famous one. A mad dwarf hero's trapped fortress, level under level under level. Generations of looters went in. The tower keeps most. What's mapped is the first quarter. It is the region's standing dare, and its vaults are real.

## The middle distances (tendays)
- **The Fields of the Dead (north).** A hundred miles of barrows and old battlefields between the Gate and Waterdeep's sphere. Restless dead, barrow treasure, and grass that grows too green over the worst spots. Caravans detour. Bounty hunters don't.
- **The Trollclaws (north, past the Winding Water).** Troll country, named honest. Trolls regrow everything but their reputation: fire finishes what blades start. The Alliance posts standing bounties per head.
- **The Wood of Sharp Teeth (east).** Vast, unlogged, unfriendly. Werewolf packs, hags in the deep, and settlements' worth of people who walked in over the years.
- **Dragonspear Castle (north, on the Trade Way).** Ruined fortress, a closed portal to Avernus in its cellars that never closed all the way. Devil cults squat there between purges.
- **Boareskyr Bridge.** Where Cyric slew Bhaal in the Time of Troubles. The water still runs wrong-colored. Oaths sworn on the bridge stick harder; the locals swear it and they are right.

## The deep board (for later, or for the bold)
- **The Anauroch desert, the High Moor, the Serpent Hills.** Dead empires under sand and moor. Iymrith's country, the blue dragon of the storms.
- **The Sea of Swords.** Pirate isles, the Nelanther. Ship work, ship crime, Umberlee's full jurisdiction. The Board pays for named pirates.
- **The Underdark.** Doors everywhere: cellar cracks, cave mouths, the bricked arch under a certain fish warehouse. Menzoberranzan's drow, duergar slave roads, and things with no surface name. Coin down there buys anything. The exchange rate is blood.

## Monsters as economy
The Lords' Alliance and merchant costers post standing marks: proof of kill (head, hide, signet) redeemable at any chartered city. See the Board in state/bounty.md. A monster mark pays coin AND witnesses: gate guards count heads out loud, broadsheets print them, and the Board learns your name. The wild is the fastest honest ladder to legend the Realms sell.

## Travel truths
- Roads: ~25 miles a day on foot, 30 by cart, weather permitting. Off road: half, and the land gets a vote.
- Nights out need watches. Fires draw eyes. No fire draws worse in some country.
- A tenday out means a tenday of world beats back home. The city does not wait (world.md clock runs regardless).
