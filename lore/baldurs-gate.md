# BALDUR'S GATE — the city, 1495 DR

Population ~125,000 plus uncounted Outer City thousands. The biggest port on the Sword Coast below Waterdeep. Built on steep hills where the River Chionthar meets the sea road. Motto in practice: everything is for sale and nothing is forgiven.

## The three cities
- **Upper City.** Old money behind its own wall. The patriars (hereditary noble families: Vanthampur, Caldwell, Hhune, Oathoon, Portyr, Ravengard by elevation) live here. The Watch polices it, gates close at dusk, non-residents need business or a bribe. Merdou gets stopped on sight here after dark.
- **Lower City.** The working port. Docks, warehouses, taverns, crime. Policed (loosely) by the Flaming Fist. Streets: Stormshore, Bindle, Cliffgate, the Steeps. This is Merdou's world.
- **Outer City.** Outside the walls, outside the law. Slums, stockyards, refugee camps (Elturel refugees since 1492), the districts of Rivington, Blackgate, Twin Songs, Whitkeep. No police at all. Little Calimshan: walled district of Calishite immigrants, its own rules.

## Power
- **Council of Four (the Grand Dukes).** Rules the city. 1495 seats: **Ulder Ravengard** (war hero, Marshal of the Flaming Fist, the real power), **Dillard Portyr** (old, tired, patriar money), one seat held by patriar compromise candidate **Thalamra-successor vacancy politics**, one seat empty since Stelmane died. A short-handed council means decisions stall and coin decides.
- **The Parliament of Peers.** Fifty patriars who advise, scheme and buy.
- **Flaming Fist.** Mercenary company under city contract, ~2,000 strong. Marshal: Ravengard. They police the Lower City like an occupying army: bribable at the bottom, brutal when embarrassed. Heat 6+ means Fist raids.
- **The Watch.** Upper City only. Unbribable by reputation, patriar-owned in fact.

## Crime
- **The Guild.** The only syndicate. Every pickpocket, fence, bookmaker and knee-breaker pays up the chain to **Nine-Fingers Keene**. Kingpins run territories under her: the Undercellar, the docks, Little Calimshan, the Outer City rackets. The Guild's rule since the crisis: independents get one warning, then an example. Merdou operating means Merdou versus this, eventually.
- **The Undercellar.** Real place: a cellar network under the Lower City, part brothel, part market, part Guild court. Neutral ground by Guild law.
- Fighting pits, unlicensed, pay Guild tax or burn. Jhessail Bilgewater's pit pays late.

## Places worth knowing
- **Elfsong Tavern.** Lower City legend. A dead elf woman's voice sings in it at odd hours. Guild-friendly ground.
- **The Low Lantern.** A three-masted ship permanently moored, now a tavern, gambling den and worse, deck by deck.
- **The Blushing Mermaid.** Outer City. The roughest famous bar on the coast. Business done here has no witnesses on purpose.
- **Wyrm's Rock.** Flaming Fist fortress on an island in the river. Prison levels below.
- **Basilisk Gate, Baldur's Gate (the actual gate), Cliffgate.** The city's mouths. Toll men, smugglers' arithmetic.
- **The Wide.** The great market. By day commerce, by night empty and patrolled.
- **Candulhallow's.** Funeral house with Guild contracts. Bodies enter with paperwork.

## Money
Copper (cp) → silver (sp, 10cp) → gold (gp, 10sp). A dock laborer earns ~2sp a day. A room in a Lower City flophouse: 5cp a night. A longsword: 15gp. A dock warehouse's monthly protection: 30-60gp to the Guild. Merdou owns 6cp.
