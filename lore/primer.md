# THE REALMS IN ONE PAGE (for a man who knows LOTR and BG3)

Out-of-game cheat sheet. Read it whenever you like; nothing in here spoils anything.

## The world
The Forgotten Realms is the world Baldur's Gate 3 happens in. The continent is called **Faerûn**. Think Middle-earth if the elves never sailed away, the Shire had street gangs, and every city ran on coin, gods and gossip. Magic is common the way literacy is common: most people have none, every city has professionals, and nobody doubts it exists.

## The gods are real and they answer
Middle-earth's powers watch from far away. Faerûn's gods take calls. Priests work miracles daily (for a price), temples are power blocks, and a god may take a personal interest in a mortal. That interest is not always good news. Withers, Shar and the Dead Three in BG3 were this system showing its teeth.

## Your city
**Baldur's Gate** is the city from BG3, three years after you saved it or watched someone else do it. It has three layers:
- **Upper City:** the old-money nobles, called **patriars**. Walls, gardens, contempt.
- **Lower City:** the docks, the trades, the pits, the mud. You live here.
- **Outer City:** the sprawl outside the walls, poorest of all. Refugees, stockyards, smoke.

## Who runs things (one line each)
- **The Guild:** the thieves' mafia that taxes every crime in the city. Nine-Fingers Keene runs it; you met her in BG3.
- **The Flaming Fist:** mercenaries who work as the city watch. Cops you can bribe. Wyll's father commands them.
- **The patriars:** the noble families. They own the courts, the walls and most of the money.
- **The Harpers:** do-gooder spies, Jaheira's people. Everywhere, thin, patient.
- **The Zhentarim:** the black-market network. Smugglers with ambition and ledgers.
- **The Lords' Alliance:** the league of cities (Baldur's Gate, Waterdeep and others) that prints the bounty posters.

## The wider map
The **Sword Coast** is the western seaboard, the campaign's main board. North of you sits **Waterdeep** (bigger and richer than the Gate), further north the pirate city **Luskan**. South lie **Amn** and **Calimshan**, merchant empires. Under everything runs the **Underdark**, the endless cave-world from BG3's first act. Moria, if Moria never ended and things down there built kingdoms.

## Three years ago
The events of BG3 are recent history here and everyone remembers: the cult of the Absolute, the mind flayers, the brain over the harbor. The heroes and monsters of that story (Karlach, Astarion and the rest) are alive, famous, and walking around with bounty numbers like celebrities. The city rebuilt crooked, and watching criminals do gods' work gave the whole coast a taste for bounty posters.

## Small practical things
- **Money:** copper, then silver (10 copper), then gold (10 silver). A loaf of bread costs 2 copper. A dock hand earns about 3 silver a day. You own 6 copper.
- **A tenday** is this world's week: ten days. The month you are in is **Flamerule**, high summer. The year is 1495 DR.
- **Tieflings** (you) carry devil blood from generations back: horns, strange eyes, fire in the line. Karlach's people, the Elturel refugees, filled the Gate with them. Some folk trust the horns less than they should say out loud.
- **Dragons** exist and are the opposite of dumb beasts: Smaug with a bank account and a long game. The oldest ones move nations without leaving their caves.
- **Haki** is the new thing, four years old: raw willpower pressed into the world. Fighters who cannot be snuck up on, fists that hurt ghosts, a presence that folds knees. Nobody knows why it started. You will meet it before anyone explains it.

## What you never need to know
Rules, numbers, spell names, monster manuals. That is the GM's machine. You say what Merdou does; the world answers honestly.
