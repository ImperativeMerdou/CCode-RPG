# GODS AND DRAGONS — the thrones Merdou is climbing toward

Gods in the Realms are real, present and political. Clerics get spells daily. Temples are power blocs. And thrones change owners: see timeline.md, Time of Troubles.

## The war portfolio (currently occupied)
- **Tempus, Lord of Battles.** THE war god. Worshipped by every soldier on both sides of every line. Doctrine: war honest and glorious, no cowardice, no war without end. His church is everywhere armies are.
- **Garagos the Reaver.** The war god Tempus beat. Once held the whole portfolio; now a minor god of plunder and bloodlust, worshipped by berserkers and pirates. **The precedent that war gods can be dethroned, and the likeliest first rung on Merdou's ladder: a diminished throne is still a throne.**
- **The Red Knight.** Goddess of strategy, Tempus's adopted daughter. War as chess.
- **Bane.** Returned god of tyranny and conquest. War as ownership. His church would love a rising warlord exactly until it tried to collar him.
- **Bhaal.** Returned god of murder. Born in Baldur's Gate's shadow; the city was named for Balduran but Bhaal's blood soaked it twice (the Bhaalspawn crisis of 1368, the Absolute's murders of 1492). Murder done in the Gate is prayer whether the killer means it or not.

## The dragon thrones
- **Tiamat, the Dragon Queen.** Five heads, goddess of chromatic dragons, greed and tyranny. Imprisoned in the Nine Hells; nearly freed in 1489. Her cult is patient. A mortal ascending toward dragon-godhood is either her instrument or her rival, and she will decide which before he does.
- **Bahamut, the Platinum Dragon.** God of metallic dragons and justice. Watches. Judges. His agents sometimes walk as old men with canaries.
- **Null / the Void Dragon.** Dragon god of death. Keeps the dragon dead. Quiet. Hungry.
- **Io, the Ninefold Dragon.** The dead overgod of all dragonkind, shattered in myth into Tiamat and Bahamut. **Table canon: the Prophecy mark on Merdou's skin is a fragment of Io's unfinished writing. Dead gods leave wills. Someone has to execute them.**

## Ascension precedents (the case law)
- **Tchazzar.** Red dragon of Chessenta who achieved godhood through worship and slaughter, died, returned. Proof a dragon can climb to divinity.
- **Cyric and Kelemvor.** Mortals who took god-thrones in 1358 with their hands.
- **The Cult of the Dragon.** Believes dragons should rule the world as god-kings; splintered since 1489 between undead-dragon orthodoxy and Tiamat worship. **A third heresy waiting to happen: a living mortal BECOMING the dragon god. They would kill or crown Merdou depending on which prophet reads him.**

## Gods a Lower City man deals with weekly
- **Tymora** (luck; gamblers' coin-flip), **Umberlee** (the sea's cruelty; sailors pay her before every voyage), **Ilmater** (endurance of the suffering; the poor's god), **Kelemvor** (honest death; funeral rites), **Mask** (thieves, allegedly dead, still prayed to in the Undercellar), **Hoar** (vengeance; you hear his name where debts go unpaid), **Loviatar** (pain; certain establishments), **Selûne and Shar** (moon and darkness, the oldest feud in creation).
