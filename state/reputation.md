# REPUTATION — three cities, three Merdous

The bounty is one number. Belief is not. Each public holds its own picture of him, fed by different papers, and the pictures diverge. Play the gaps: a hero of the Outer City can be a monster in the Upper and a rumor in between. Update lines when deeds or broadsheets touch a public.

| Public | Paper that feeds it | What they believe about Merdou now |
|---|---|---|
| Lower City (docks, pits, taverns) | **The Baldur's Mouth** (gutter, lurid, first to print) | A giant tiefling nobody knows walked into Bilgewater's and threw the Brick over his head like a grain sack. The retelling grows by the hour; no name attached yet. |
| Upper City (patriars, the Watch) | **The Parliament Ledger** (dry, patriar-owned, prints what money wants) | Does not know he exists. |
| Outer City (refugees, stockyards, the walls' shadow) | **word of mouth** (no paper; slower, truer, then wilder) | Nothing yet. |

## The rule of three papers
The Mouth exaggerates. The Ledger launders. The street corrects both, two tendays late, into legend. When his deed hits print, write what EACH public now believes, and let the beliefs disagree. NPCs act on their public's version, never on the truth.
