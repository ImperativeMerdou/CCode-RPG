# REPUTATION — three cities, three Merdous

The bounty is one number. Belief is not. Each public holds its own picture of him, fed by different papers, and the pictures diverge. Play the gaps: a hero of the Outer City can be a monster in the Upper and a rumor in between. Update lines when deeds or broadsheets touch a public.

| Public | Paper that feeds it | What they believe about Merdou now |
|---|---|---|
| Lower City (docks, pits, taverns) | **The Baldur's Mouth** (gutter, lurid, first to print) | After card one: the Ox knocked out their beloved Eel BLIND, and the Eel himself is buying the legend drinks. The boos are turning into a game: they boo him in the front rows and bet on him in the back. "Hits like a falling church" is already tavern-speech. |
| Upper City (patriars, the Watch) | **The Parliament Ledger** (dry, patriar-owned, prints what money wants) | Does not know he exists. Pit filth is beneath the Ledger. |
| Outer City (refugees, stockyards, the walls' shadow) | **word of mouth** (no paper; slower, truer, then wilder) | A day behind and closer to true: "a HORNED giant, tiefling-blood, not orc, whatever the paper says." The horns are growing in each telling. |

## The rule of three papers
The Mouth exaggerates. The Ledger launders. The street corrects both, two tendays late, into legend. When his deed hits print, write what EACH public now believes, and let the beliefs disagree. NPCs act on their public's version, never on the truth.
