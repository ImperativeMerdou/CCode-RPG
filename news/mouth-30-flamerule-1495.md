**THE BALDUR'S MOUTH** — *2 eyes* — 30 Flamerule, 1495 DR (Godsday)

## THE RUIN AND THE RISE OF THE OX — AN EXCLUSIVE, BY S. CHAINMAN

He sat across from me at the table he broke with another man's body, and he talked the way he fights: straight ahead, no apologies, no guard.

MERDOU. No family name offered and none needed. Tiefling, twenty-seven, tall as a door and a half. He has eaten every gutter this coast keeps and some it hides: the dens, the poison years, the wreckage a man does to himself when the world marks him at birth and dares him to mind. He minded. He burned. And then, readers, he stopped burning and started climbing, and the Gate is only now noticing what's coming up the ladder.

He knocked out the Brick with a throw that broke furniture. He knocked out our own beloved Eel stone blind, swinging at the sound of a smile. Tonight, Godsday, he fights again at the usual cellar, and this paper, first in the city, tells you plainly: go and see him now, while the standing room is still priced for dockhands. Men like this stop being affordable.

Asked what he wants, he looked at me a long time and I will be honest with you, readers, I wrote three answers and crossed out all three. Some wants don't fit in a column.

The Mouth reminds you that pit fighting is illegal, that Midsummer is tomorrow, and that this correspondent has seen the future of the Gate's legend trade, and it has horns.
